What Types of Civil Suits Does MR Law Associates Handle?

MR Law Associates handles all categories of civil litigation filed under the Code of Civil Procedure 1908 (CPC) in Karachi's Civil Courts and the High Court of Sindh.

The firm accepts cases involving monetary recovery, property rights, contractual enforcement, and declaratory relief across all civil court jurisdictions in Karachi.

The four primary categories of civil suits we handle include:

Recovery Suits (Order XXXVII CPC)

Filing and defending suits for recovery of money, damages, and outstanding financial dues. This includes cheque dishonor recovery, loan repayment disputes, rent arrears, and breach-of-contract damages. Summary procedures under Order XXXVII allow faster resolution for claims based on written instruments.

Injunction Applications (Order XXXIX CPC)

Obtaining temporary and permanent injunctions to prevent unauthorized actions. Common uses include stopping construction on disputed land, preventing property transfers during litigation, restraining parties from disposing of assets, and protecting possession rights. Interim injunctions can be granted within days of filing.

Declaratory Decrees (Specific Relief Act 1877)

Establishing legal ownership, rights, and status through court-issued declarations. Property ownership disputes, inheritance claims, partnership rights, and title disputes are resolved through declaratory suits. The court's declaration carries binding legal force on all parties.

Specific Performance (Specific Relief Act 1877)

Enforcing contractual obligations when monetary compensation is inadequate. If a seller refuses to transfer property after receiving payment, or a partner refuses to honor a business agreement, the court can order specific performance, compelling the defaulting party to fulfill their contractual obligations exactly.

How Does the Civil Suit Filing Process Work in Karachi?

Filing a civil suit in Karachi follows a structured procedure governed by the Code of Civil Procedure 1908 and the specific rules of the Sindh Civil Courts. The process begins with a legal consultation, proceeds through documentation, filing, summons, and ultimately trial or settlement.

Here is the step-by-step process:

  1. Initial Consultation: You present your case facts and documents to your advocate. Reema Jawed evaluates the legal merit, identifies the cause of action, and advises on the probability of success.
  2. Plaint Drafting: Your advocate prepares the plaint (the formal complaint). This document includes the facts of the case, the cause of action, the relief sought, and the legal basis under applicable statutes.
  3. Court Fee Calculation: Court fees are calculated based on the Court Fees Act 1870. Property dispute cases and recovery suits carry ad valorem fees (percentage of claimed amount). Declaratory suits have fixed fees.
  4. Filing and Registration: The plaint is filed in the appropriate Civil Court in Karachi. The court assigns a case number and issues summons to the defendant.
  5. Written Statement by Defendant: The defendant files their response within 30 days (extendable). This outlines their defense and any counterclaims.
  6. Framing of Issues: The court identifies the key legal and factual points to be decided. Both sides agree on the issues for trial.
  7. Evidence and Arguments: Both parties present witnesses, cross-examine, and submit documentary evidence under the Qanun-e-Shahadat Order 1984.
  8. Judgment: The court delivers its decision. If either party disagrees, they can file an appeal before the High Court of Sindh within 30 days.

Why Should You Hire a Civil Suit Lawyer Instead of Self-Representation?

Self-representation in civil suits is legally permitted but statistically disadvantageous. Courts in Pakistan follow strict procedural requirements under the Code of Civil Procedure 1908 and the Qanun-e-Shahadat Order 1984 (evidence law).

A single procedural error, including incorrect plaint format, improper service of summons, or missed limitation deadlines, can lead to suit dismissal without a hearing on merits.

A qualified civil suit lawyer in Karachi provides:

  • Procedural compliance: Correct filing procedures, proper court fee stamps, and timely submissions prevent technical dismissals.
  • Jurisdiction analysis: Determining the correct court (Civil Judge, Senior Civil Judge, or District Court) based on claim value and subject matter.
  • Evidence strategy: Organizing documentary and oral evidence for maximum impact at trial.
  • Cross-examination: Experienced advocates identify inconsistencies in opposing witness testimony that strengthen your case.
  • Negotiation leverage: Many civil suits settle before judgment. A skilled advocate negotiates favorable settlement terms by leveraging the strength of your filed case.
  • Appeal preparation: If the trial court's decision is unfavorable, the advocate files an appeal before the High Court of Sindh within the statutory 30-day limitation period.

What Is the Limitation Period for Filing a Civil Suit in Pakistan?

The Limitation Act 1908 sets fixed deadlines for filing civil suits in Pakistan. If a suit is filed after the limitation period expires, the court dismisses it automatically.

The limitation period begins from the date the cause of action arises, meaning the date you first become aware of the legal wrong.

Type of Suit Limitation Period Starts From
Recovery of money (contract) 3 years Date payment became due
Specific performance of contract 3 years Date of breach
Declaratory suit (property) 6 years Date right first denied
Possession of immovable property 12 years Date of dispossession
Cheque bounce recovery 3 years Date of cheque dishonor
Tort/damages (negligence) 1 year Date of injury or damage

Missing the limitation deadline is final. Courts lack discretion to extend these periods except in cases where the plaintiff proves they were unable to file due to fraud, mistake, or disability.

Consult an advocate immediately if your limitation period is approaching to protect your legal rights.

How long does a civil suit take in Karachi courts?

A civil suit in Karachi typically takes 2 to 5 years to reach final judgment, depending on case complexity, number of witnesses, and court backlog.

Cases with injunction applications can receive interim relief within weeks. Summary suits under Order XXXVII may conclude faster.

What is the court fee for filing a civil suit in Pakistan?

Court fees vary by suit type. Recovery suits require ad valorem fees calculated as a percentage of the claimed amount (typically 1-7.5%) under the Court Fees Act 1870.

Declaratory suits carry fixed fees. Your advocate calculates the exact amount based on claim value and court jurisdiction.

Can I file a civil suit without a lawyer in Karachi?

Legally, yes, appearing "in person" is permitted. Practically, civil procedure requires strict compliance with the CPC 1908, evidence law, and court-specific rules.

A single procedural error can result in dismissal. Professional representation significantly improves case outcomes.

What is the difference between a civil suit and a criminal case?

A civil suit resolves private disputes between individuals (property, contracts, money). A criminal case involves offenses against the state (theft, fraud, assault).

Civil suits result in compensation or specific relief. Criminal cases result in penalties or imprisonment.
