Frequently Asked Questions About Hiring a Lawyer in Karachi

How do I choose the best lawyer in Karachi for my case?

To choose the best lawyer in Karachi, verify their enrollment with the Sindh Bar Council, confirm they are an Advocate High Court for complex matters, check their specialization in your specific case type (civil, family, criminal), review their track record and success rate, and ensure their office location provides convenient access to relevant courts. What are the legal fees for hiring a lawyer in Karachi?

Legal fees in Karachi vary by case complexity and lawyer experience. Initial consultations typically range from PKR 5,000-15,000. Can a lawyer in Karachi help with property disputes and stay orders?

Yes, a property lawyer in Karachi can file for stay orders under Order 39 Rule 1 & 2 CPC of the Civil Procedure Code to prevent illegal dispossession. They can handle illegal possession cases under the Illegal Dispossession Act 2005, file suits for declaration to establish title, pursue partition suits for inheritance matters, and represent you in Sindh High Court for property-related constitutional petitions. Emergency stay orders can often be obtained within 24-48 hours.

How long does a civil case take in Karachi courts?

The duration of civil cases in Karachi varies by complexity. Simple recovery suits may resolve in 6-12 months, while complex property disputes can take 2-4 years. Cases involving multiple parties or requiring extensive evidence take longer. Interim relief like stay orders can be obtained quickly (24-72 hours). Our proximity to City Court allows for efficient case management and faster filing of urgent applications.

What is the difference between a lawyer and an Advocate High Court?

In Pakistan, a lawyer registered with the Pakistan Bar Council can practice in lower courts (District Courts, Family Courts). An Advocate High Court has additional qualifications and licensing to practice before the Sindh High Court and file appeals. For complex litigation, constitutional petitions, or appeals, you need an Advocate High Court. Advocate Reema Jawed is licensed to represent clients in all Karachi courts including Sindh High Court.

Can overseas Pakistanis hire a lawyer in Karachi without visiting?

Yes, overseas Pakistanis can hire a lawyer in Karachi remotely. You need to execute a Special Power of Attorney (SPA) attested at the Pakistani Embassy/Consulate in your country of residence. This document is then verified and registered in Pakistan. Your appointed lawyer can then handle property matters, divorce proceedings, inheritance cases, and litigation on your behalf without requiring your physical presence in Karachi.
