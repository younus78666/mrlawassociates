A Restitution of Conjugal Rights suit (Ada-e-Haqooq-e-Zaojiyat) is a legal action filed in a Pakistani Family Court by one spouse demanding that the other spouse physically return to the marital home and resume marital obligations. MR Law Associates frequently defends wives in Karachi against these lawsuits, as husbands routinely weaponize this statute to deliberately block the wife’s legitimate claims for monthly maintenance and child support.

Marriage under Islamic law is a civil contract that guarantees specific mutual rights. When a wife leaves the home—often due to domestic hostility—the husband almost immediately files this suit in the Karachi Family Courts. It is crucial to understand that no Pakistani court will ever use police force to physically drag a woman back to her husband. The true objective of this lawsuit is entirely financial: to legally brand the wife as "disobedient" so the court extinguishes her right to receive monthly maintenance (Nafqa).

Why Are These Suits Filed? The Retaliatory Trap

Very rarely is a restitution suit filed out of genuine love. It is almost always a highly calculated, retaliatory legal maneuver engineered by the opposing counsel.

Blocking Maintenance (Kharcha)

If a wife files a legal suit demanding her legally guaranteed monthly maintenance, the husband’s immediate counter-move is to file for Restitution. If the judge grants his decree and the wife refuses to return, she forfeits her own maintenance (though child maintenance remains strictly unaffected).

Defeating Khula Timelines

When a wife seeks a Khula (fast-track divorce), husbands file for Restitution simply to complicate the case file, attempting to drag out the Family Court proceedings by forcing the judge to consolidate and hear multiple suits simultaneously.

What Happens if the Husband Wins the Decree?

This is the most common fear for women facing this litigation. It is vital to understand the limits of judicial power in Family Courts.

Even if the Husband wins the Restitution of Conjugal Rights decree, the Family Court cannot issue execution warrants to physically force the wife back. The only legal penalty enforced against the wife is that she loses her right to claim past and future spousal maintenance (Nafqa) from him. Furthermore, the husband’s obligation to provide child maintenance remains 100% active; he cannot use a restitution decree to stop paying for his children's food or schooling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the police force me to go back to my husband?

Absolutely not. Under no circumstances can the police, a bailiff, or a Family Court Judge physically compel a woman to return to her husband’s house against her will, regardless of the court decree.

Why did my husband file this right after I asked for Kharcha?

It is a standard retaliatory tactic. He is attempting to prove you are a 'disobedient wife' who left without cause, solely so the Judge will dismiss your maintenance (Kharcha) lawsuit.

How does "Prompt Dower" defend against this suit?

If your Nikah Nama specifies "Prompt (Moajjal)" dower and your husband has not paid it, you hold the supreme legal right to deny conjugal relations until he pays it. We use this to instantly collapse his restitution suit.

Does refusing to return affect my child custody rights?

No. Your right to Hizanat (child custody) is judged entirely on the welfare of the minor. Refusing to live with an abusive husband does not legally disqualify you from being an excellent mother, nor does it revoke your custody rights.

Should I file for Khula if he files for restitution?

Yes, highly recommended. If you have zero intention of returning, we actively file a counter-suit for Khula. The court will consolidate both cases and almost universally grant the Khula, rendering his restitution suit legally dead.
