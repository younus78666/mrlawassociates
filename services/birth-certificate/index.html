Late entry requires swearing a legal affidavit, compiling supporting evidence (school records, CNICs), and securing official approval from the Deputy Commissioner (DC) before the Union Council can generate the certificate.

How Are Birth Certificates Issued via NADRA CRMS?

Historically, birth certificates were handwritten municipal registers. Today, Pakistan uses the centralized Civil Registration Management System (CRMS) linked directly to the NADRA database. The Union Council acts as the data entry point for NADRA.

  1. Application submission: Parents apply at their local residential Union Council, submitting the hospital discharge summary and parents' CNIC copies.
  2. Union Council data entry: The UC Secretary inputs the data into the CRMS terminal. The system links the newborn's identity to the parents' CNIC family tree.
  3. CRMS verification: The system automatically verifies the parents' citizenship status and current marital linkage.
  4. Certificate printing: Once approved, the Union Council prints the standardized computerized birth certificate featuring a unique tracking number and municipal barcode.
  5. Final sign-off: The authorized Union Council officer signs and officially stamps the document, making it a valid state record.

What Documents Are Necessary for Processing?

Submitting a complete file on the first attempt prevents bureaucratic rejection. The required evidence changes significantly if applying for a late entry.

  • Newborn (Normal): Original hospital discharge certificate or clinic birth slip, copies of father and mother's valid CNICs, existing family Form-B (if applicable).
  • Late Registration (Child): Hospital birth slip, child's vaccination card/records, parents' CNIC copies, and an affidavit of delay.
  • Late Registration (Adult/Unregistered): Copies of parents' CNICs (or death certificates if deceased), applicant's own CNIC, school matriculation certificate (as age proof), and a magistrate-sworn affidavit confirming birth particulars.

Frequently Asked Questions About Birth Certificates

How to apply for a birth certificate in Karachi?

Submit the hospital birth slip and parents' CNICs to the local Union Council where the birth occurred. The UC inputs data into the NADRA CRMS system and prints a computerized, verifiable certificate.

What is a late entry birth certificate?

A procedure required if a birth remains unregistered for more than six months. It involves swearing a legal affidavit, providing alternative age proof like school records, and securing approval from the Deputy Commissioner.

Can I correct my date of birth on an existing certificate?

Yes, but it is complex. Minor typographical errors are resolved administratively. Significant year modifications require filing a formal civil suit and securing a judicial decree ordering NADRA to ammend the record.

What documents are required?

For standard entry: hospital discharge slip, father and mother's CNIC copies. Without hospital records, formal witness affidavits and supplementary medical checks are mandated.

What is a Non-Availability Certificate (NOC)?

An official municipal declaration stating no birth record exists for an individual. It serves as secondary proof of birth particulars, predominantly used to satisfy US, Canadian, and UK immigration requirements regarding legacy birth records.

How long does processing take?

Normal registration timeline: 1 to 3 working days. Late entry registration with DC approval: 15 to 30 days. Legal date of birth correction via court decree: 3 to 6 months.
