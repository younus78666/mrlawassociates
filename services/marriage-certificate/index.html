How Does Marriage Registration Work in Karachi?

Marriage registration in Pakistan follows a defined legal process under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961 and the Muslim Marriages and Divorces (Registration) Act. The process involves two stages: nikah registration with the Union Council and NADRA certificate issuance.

  1. Nikah ceremony: The nikah is performed by a licensed nikah khwan (marriage registrar) in the presence of two male witnesses or one male and two female witnesses. Both bride and groom (or their wakeel) provide consent.
  2. Nikah nama signing: The nikah nama (marriage contract) is signed by both parties, witnesses, and the nikah khwan. It specifies haq mehr (dower) amount, conditions, and special stipulations.
  3. Union Council registration: The nikah khwan submits the nikah nama to the local Union Council within 7 days. The Union Council issues a certified registered nikah nama.
  4. CNIC update: Both spouses visit NADRA to update their CNICs with spouse details (marital status change from single to married).
  5. Marriage certificate: NADRA issues a computerized marriage certificate based on the registered nikah nama and updated CNIC records.

What Documents Are Required for a Marriage Certificate?

  • Registered nikah nama from Union Council (original + copies)
  • CNIC of both bride and groom (original + copies)
  • CNIC copies of two witnesses who attended the nikah
  • Passport-size photographs of both spouses
  • Previous marriage documents (if applicable — divorce decree or death certificate of previous spouse)
  • Affidavit (for court marriage or late registration)

What Is a Court Marriage and When Is It Needed?

A court marriage is performed before a Magistrate or civil authority and provides immediate legal recognition. Court marriages are used in specific situations where a traditional nikah ceremony is not feasible or where additional legal protection is needed.

Common situations requiring court marriage:

  • Family opposition: When families do not consent to the marriage. Pakistani law allows adults (18+) to marry without family consent.
  • Inter-sect marriage: Marriages between different Islamic sects where getting a nikah khwan from one sect may cause issues.
  • Immediate legal documentation: When an officially recognized certificate is needed urgently for travel, visa, or other purposes.
  • Second marriage: When a husband is contracting a second marriage (requiring Union Council permission under Section 6 of the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance).

Frequently Asked Questions About Marriage Certificates

How do I get a marriage certificate in Karachi?

Steps: (1) nikah by licensed nikah khwan, (2) nikah nama registered at Union Council, (3) CNIC update at NADRA, (4) NADRA computerized marriage certificate. For foreign use, get MOFA attestation.

How long does a NADRA marriage certificate take?

Regular service: 7 to 21 working days. Executive service: 3 to 7 working days. Timeline depends on whether nikah is already registered with Union Council and document completeness.

What is a court marriage in Pakistan?

A nikah performed before a Magistrate with immediate legal recognition. Used for family opposition cases, inter-sect marriages, and when urgent official documentation is needed. Both parties (18+) must appear with witnesses and CNICs.

Can I register a marriage performed abroad?

Yes. Register at the Pakistan Embassy/Consulate in the country of marriage. The embassy certificate can then be used for NADRA CNIC update and marriage certificate issuance in Pakistan.

What if my nikah nama was never registered?

Late registration is possible through a court affidavit and Union Council application. We handle the complete process including witness statements, affidavit drafting, and Union Council coordination.

What documents are needed?

Required: registered nikah nama, CNICs of both spouses, CNIC copies of witnesses, passport photos. For court marriage: additional affidavits. For remarriage: divorce decree or death certificate.
