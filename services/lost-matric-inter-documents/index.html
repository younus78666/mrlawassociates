What Are the Legal Prerequisites for a Duplicate Certificate?

Before submitting an application form to the educational board, three distinct legal actions must be completed to prove the document was lost and to indemnify the board against misuse.

Police Report (FIR / Roznamcha)

Filing a formal report at the police station in the jurisdiction where the document was lost. The report must specifically detail which document was lost (Matric/Inter), the passing year, roll number, and the applicant's name. The board requires an attested copy of this report.

Newspaper Publication

Publishing a lost document notice in a daily newspaper approved by the board (usually one Urdu and one English daily). The notice alerts the public that the original document is voided and protects you if someone attempts to misuse your credentials.

Magistrate Affidavit

Swearing a legal affidavit before a First Class Magistrate or an Oath Commissioner. This sworn statement declares under penalty of perjury that the document was genuinely lost, has not been pledged as a guarantee, and guarantees return of the original if ever found.

Board Application Submission

Combining the police report, newspaper clipping, affidavit, CNIC copies, and paid fee vouchers into a bound file and submitting it to the specific verification counter at BSEK (Nazimabad) or BIEK (North Nazimabad).

Which Educational Boards Do We Coordinate With?

Karachi features multiple educational jurisdictions, each with entirely distinct duplicate issuance SOPs, fee structures, and application portals.

  • BSEK (Board of Secondary Education Karachi): Responsible for 9th and 10th grade (Matriculation) certificates for Science and General groups in Karachi.
  • BIEK (Board of Intermediate Education Karachi): Responsible for 11th and 12th grade (Intermediate) certificates spanning Pre-Medical, Pre-Engineering, Commerce, and Humanities.
  • SBTTE (Sindh Board of Technical Education): Covers DAE (Diploma of Associate Engineering) technical diplomas and vocational training certificates.

What If You Need to Correct an Error on the Duplicate?

Often, individuals discover errors (name spelling, father's name, date of birth) on their original certificates only after losing them. You cannot request a duplicate and a correction simultaneously. The standard legal procedure dictates you must first obtain the duplicate bearing the existing error, and subsequently initiate a formal correction procedure—often requiring a civil suit for declaration if changing the date of birth or parentage significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Duplicate Certificates

How do I get a duplicate matric certificate?

Complete three legal prerequisites: police FIR, newspaper notice, and magistrate affidavit. Submit these with the application form to the Board of Secondary Education Karachi (BSEK).

Which boards issue these duplicates?

BSEK (Nazimabad) handles 9th and 10th-grade (Matric) certificates. BIEK (North Nazimabad) manages 11th and 12th-grade (Intermediate) credentials for Karachi-based students.

How long does the duplicate process take?

Standard processing: 15 to 30 working days after submitting the complete legal file. Urgent processing: 7 to 10 working days (requires a higher bank fee voucher).

What documents do I need to supply?

Mandatory: CNIC copy, any photocopy of the lost record (for roll number reference), and photographs. We handle creating the remaining legal prerequisites on your behalf.

Do I need a lawyer for this?

While not strictly mandatory, a lawyer efficiently executes the FIR, newspaper ad, and magistrate affidavit, saving you weeks of navigating police stations and confusing board requirements.

Are duplicates accepted for university or visas?

Yes. An officially issued duplicate from BSEK or BIEK carries the exact same legal weight as the original and is completely authorized for HEC attestation and immigration assessments.
