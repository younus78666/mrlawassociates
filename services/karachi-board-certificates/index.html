What Is the Required Attestation Sequence?

You cannot simply take a Matric certificate directly to MOFA or IBCC. Pakistan enforces a strict, unbreakable chronological sequence for verifying secondary educational documents.

1. Issuing Board Verification

The initial and most crucial step. The original issuing authority (e.g., BSEK for Matric, BIEK for Inter) inspects their internal registers to confirm the document's authenticity. They seal their verification confirmation in an official board envelope. IBCC will not accept unsealed or already opened verifications.

2. IBCC Attestation

The Inter Board Committee of Chairmen is the federal regulatory body resolving equivalence and attesting all secondary certificates nationwide. You present your original certificates alongside the sealed verification envelope from the Karachi board. IBCC fixes their specific holographic sticker and stamp directly onto the original certificate.

3. MOFA Apostille / Attestation

Finally, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs verifies the signature of the authorized IBCC officer. MOFA attestation (or Apostille) is the ultimate authentication stamp, rendering the Pakistani educational document legally acceptable to foreign consulates and international employers.

4. Foreign Consulate (If required)

For Middle Eastern jurisdictions (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar), a final attestation from the specific country's embassy or consulate located in Pakistan is frequently required following MOFA's clearance.

WES Sealed Envelopes for USA/Canada Immigration

Organizations like World Education Services (WES), ICAS, or IQAS (for Canadian PR via Express Entry) do not require IBCC/MOFA stamps. However, they enforce a distinct "sealed envelope" requirement.

The authorized officer then stamps across the sealed flap and signs it. We then manage the dispatched courier directly from the board's premises to WES in North America, ensuring total compliance.

Frequently Asked Questions About Board Attestations

What is the verification sequence?

The unbreakable chain: 1. Issuing Board (BSEK/BIEK) → 2. IBCC Attestation → 3. MOFA Apostille/Attestation. Bypassing any step results in immediate rejection at the next authority.

What does a sealed envelope for WES mean?

World Education Services mandates the verified certificate copies be placed in an official board envelope, which is then legally stamped and signed across the reverse flap to prove the evaluation is untampered.

Can IBCC attest laminated certificates?

Absolutely not. IBCC and MOFA officials must apply their holographic stickers and ink stamps directly to the original certificate paper fiber. Professional delamination is required prior to submission.

What if my name doesn't match my CNIC?

IBCC will systematically reject the file. You must legally synchronize your ID data by either correcting NADRA records or undertaking a board-level correction (often requiring legal intervention) before seeking attestation.

How long does the entire process take?

Completing the full sequence (Board + IBCC + MOFA) typically absorbs 3 to 4 weeks depending on agency backlogs. Rushing the process invariably leads to errors.

Do you handle foreign consulate attestations?

Yes. Once MOFA has cleared the documents, we manage the final submission to Middle Eastern consulates (UAE, Saudi, Qatar) operating inside Pakistan.
